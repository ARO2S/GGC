On Tuesday evening, June 9, members of the Greenville Garden Club gathered at Emerald Pointe on Honey Locust Lane for the June program — an evening dedicated to one of gardening's most rewarding challenges: working with shade.

Pat Kious led the presentation, walking members through the art of gardening in shaded spaces. From choosing the right plants to understanding the different types of shade your yard might offer — dappled, deep, dry, or moist — Pat shared practical knowledge that anyone with a shady corner (or a mostly shady yard) can put to use.

![Shade gardening presentation by Pat Kious](/images/uploads/ShadeGardening-1.jpeg)

Shade gardening often gets a reputation for being limiting, but an evening like this is a good reminder that shaded spaces have their own palette of textures, colors, and quietly beautiful plants that simply wouldn't survive in full sun. Hostas, ferns, astilbe, hellebores, and bleeding heart are just a few of the plants that genuinely thrive once the sun steps aside.

Thank you to Pat for sharing her expertise, and to Emerald Pointe for hosting such a lovely evening setting for the program.
